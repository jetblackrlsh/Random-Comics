# Character Bible

## Global Photo-Comic Look

Every page should look like a full realistic candid photo-comic page assembled from documentary, handheld, phone-photo, or photojournalistic images. The VFX should feel photographed in real environments: glowing quantum particles, photoreal laser light, time distortion, electrical barriers, and wave motion integrated into believable lighting and camera grain. Use clean rectangular caption boxes only, with all readable text generated directly inside the page art.

## Particle Patriot

- Role: Humanoid superhero from the Quantum Realm, working with the United States Government.
- Body: Entire figure made from distinct baseball sized atoms, each glossy and physical, colored red, white, or blue. The atoms cluster into a heroic human silhouette with a clear head, torso, arms, and legs, but no normal skin, hair, fabric, or conventional costume.
- Face: Suggested by the arrangement of particles, with glowing white-blue eye clusters and a confident heroic expression made from particle spacing.
- Motion states: Can scatter into individual red, white, and blue particles; reform as a humanoid in midair; stretch into a luminous wave-shaped stream of particles; focus electrons into a crackling blue-white electrical barrier.
- Signature visual motif: Patriotic red, white, and blue particle body, quantum shimmer, tiny orbital trails around the atoms, and occasional close-ups of quantum particles visible through his Quantum Vision.
- Continuity: The body remains made entirely of baseball sized atoms on every page, even when stretched, scattered, or compressed. Do not add a fabric suit, cape, helmet, human face, or normal body parts.

## The Clocktor

- Role: Villainous super-genius and thief, formerly a brilliant student of quantum time theory.
- Age and build: Middle-aged man, lean, intense posture, pale tired face, sharp cheekbones, wild gray-streaked dark hair.
- Outfit: Long charcoal coat over a burgundy waistcoat, brass pocket-watch chains, black gloves, dark trousers, scuffed boots, and brass clockwork goggles pushed up on his forehead.
- Props: Prototype time displacement ray shaped like a heavy retro-futuristic brass-and-steel rifle with glowing blue chronal coils and a spinning clock-dial emitter.
- Expression range: Grandiose confidence, brittle resentment, manic focus, stunned disbelief when defeated.

## King Clocktor

- Role: Future tyrant version of Clocktor.
- Visible identifiers: Older and more imposing Clocktor with a high black-and-gold collar, cracked brass crown made from clock hands, glowing blue chronal scars around one eye, and a throne built from giant gears, clocks, and broken museum pieces.
- Setting motif: Dystopian wasteland filled with mixed eras: dinosaur bones, rusted cars, broken Roman columns, neon signs, gears, and futuristic ruins.

## Temporal Drones

- Role: Enforcers of King Clocktor's future.
- Design: Brass-and-black hovering drones shaped like clock faces with articulated metal wings, rotating hands, blue optic lenses, and thin laser barrels.
- Behavior: Move in disciplined swarms, fire blue-red laser blasts, and shatter when their own shots are reflected.

## American Museum of Timekeeping

- Present-day setting: A grand museum hall with marble floors, glass display cases, tall banners about clocks and calendars, and the world's largest golden hourglass as the central exhibit.
- Golden hourglass: Monumental polished gold frame, taller than a person, with glowing sand and armored glass supports.
