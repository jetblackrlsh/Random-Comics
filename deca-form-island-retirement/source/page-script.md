# Page Script

Global style for every page: full realistic candid photo-comic page, 4:5 portrait aspect ratio, complete page with multiple panels or a splash panel, photoreal documentary phone-photo or handheld photojournalism look, natural imperfect framing, believable real-world lighting, photoreal action VFX, clean rectangular caption boxes only. No speech bubbles, no thought bubbles, no floating dialogue, no subtitles, no watermark, no logo, no blank text boxes. All readable story text must be generated directly inside the page art. This revised edition has 9 pages: cover, prologue, and 7 story pages.

## Page 01 - Cover

Story role: Signal the survival-superhero premise and the island transformation arc.

Layout: Dramatic cover-like photo-comic splash. Foreground Deca-Form in human form stands on a moonlit beach, wet and sand-covered, belt glowing silver, neon red, and metallic black. Behind him, the wrecked cruise ship is split by lightning in the far ocean, while ghosted silhouettes of butterfly wings, speedster jets, plant vines, and amoeba duplicates radiate around him.

Caption/text inside art: Title text: "DECA-FORM: ISLAND RETIREMENT". Small caption box: "Sometimes superheroes have to save themselves."

## Page 02 - Prologue: The Belt And The Breathing

Story role: Explain Deca-Form's identity, his alien belt powers, the ten-minute limit and recharge, his anxiety, and why he finally takes a vacation.

Layout: Six panels. Panel 1: Deca-Form standing in a quiet city alley after a rescue, belt glowing under his black leather jacket. Panel 2: close-up of the alien belt projecting ten small holographic alien silhouettes. Panel 3: one silhouette flickers with a digital ten-minute timer and recharge warning. Panel 4: Deca-Form after a heroic moment, surrounded by distant emergency lights, privately anxious and trying to breathe. Panel 5: his hand checking a cruise ticket and small packed bag. Panel 6: Deca-Form stepping onto a cruise ship deck, still tense but trying to accept rest.

Caption boxes inside art: "Deca-Form's alien belt could unlock ten different forms." "Each form lasted ten minutes. Then the belt had to recharge." "Being a hero did not make anxiety disappear." "When the fear got too loud, he finally chose a vacation."

## Page 03 - Vacation Turns

Story role: Establish Deca-Form, the cruise vacation, and the storm beginning.

Layout: Four panels. Panel 1: Deca-Form relaxed on a cruise deck in daylight. Panel 2: his alien belt close-up glowing under the black leather jacket. Panel 3: clouds slowly fill the sky over the ocean. Panel 4: dark clouds begin swirling above the ship.

Caption boxes inside art: "A weekend cruise was supposed to be the one mission with no mission." "Then the sky started making plans of its own."

## Page 04 - The Bolt

Story role: Inciting disaster.

Layout: Five urgent handheld panels. Panel 1: passengers react as the sky turns black. Panel 2: lightning builds inside a twisting cloud funnel. Panel 3: a massive bolt slams down and splits the cruise ship. Panel 4: shockwave throws Deca-Form over the railing. Panel 5: underwater blur as he sinks unconscious, belt still glowing faintly.

Caption boxes inside art: "The bolt did not strike the ship." "It judged it." "One second later, Deca-Form was gone."

## Page 05 - Alone

Story role: Wake on the island and use the butterfly form to assess the environment.

Layout: Six panels. Panel 1: Deca-Form wakes on pale sand at dawn. Panel 2: panic on his face as he sees empty ocean. Panel 3: he closes his eyes, one hand on the belt, breathing. Panel 4: transformation light wraps him. Panel 5: winged iridescent humanoid butterfly form takes off above palms. Panel 6: high aerial view showing only endless ocean.

Caption boxes inside art: "No ship. No radio. No one calling his name." "First rule: breathe." "Second rule: get above the fear."

## Page 06 - No Horizon Answers

Story role: Show the first failed rescue attempt and the danger of the ten-minute limit.

Layout: Five panels. Panel 1: Deca-Form lands disappointed and turns human beside the belt glow. Panel 2: he waits tensely for recharge. Panel 3: he transforms into the robotic jet-powered racecar speedster. Panel 4: speed form runs across the ocean surface, spray exploding behind him. Panel 5: five minutes later, only water in every direction.

Caption boxes inside art: "The sky gave him distance." "The belt gave him ten minutes." "The ocean gave him nothing back."

## Page 07 - Turn Around

Story role: Escalate anxiety and force him to abandon the rescue search.

Layout: Six panels. Panel 1: speed form running over roughening waves. Panel 2: belt timer glow dimming at his waist-core. Panel 3: Deca-Form realizes he could time out over open water. Panel 4: he reverses direction in a violent spray arc. Panel 5: collapses back on the island in human form. Panel 6: close-up of him hyperventilating, eyes shut, counting on his fingers.

Caption boxes inside art: "Halfway out, hope became math." "If the clock beat him, the ocean won." "So he ran back to the only answer he had."

## Page 08 - Build A Home

Story role: Active choice: reframe the goal from escape to survival and belonging.

Layout: Six panels. Panel 1: Deca-Form kneels in sand, calmer, watching the empty horizon. Panel 2: plant transformation blooms around him. Panel 3: rainbow flower-covered wooden vine body rises from the beach. Panel 4: bioluminescent vines knit themselves into a shelter. Panel 5: glowing seeds radiate from his hands into soil. Panel 6: a massive fruit and vegetable garden grows around the shelter.

Caption boxes inside art: "Home stopped meaning a place behind him." "It became the next thing he could make." "Shelter first. Food second. Panic last."

## Page 09 - Not Alone

Story role: Resolution: he solves loneliness, reshapes the island, and finds peace.

Layout: Large final-page montage with six panels. Panel 1: Deca-Form is completely alone in human form, with no duplicates or extra versions of him anywhere in the panel; he stands before the shelter and garden, smiles for a moment, then the smile fades as loneliness hits. Panel 2: amoeba form duplication begins, translucent figures splitting apart for the first time on this page. Panel 3: several duplicates manually detransform into Deca-Form clones. Panel 4: clones use different alien transformations to create homes, roads, gardens, and lookout towers. Panel 5: wide golden-hour view of the transformed island settlement. Panel 6: Deca-Form and clones sit together on a beach road at sunset, peaceful.

Caption boxes inside art: "Comfort was not the same as company." "So Deca-Form stopped waiting for rescue." Panel 3 caption box: "His Amoeba-Man form can duplicate. By having them all manually change back to normal: One became many." "He made enough of himself to build a life." "Sometimes superheroes have to save themselves."
