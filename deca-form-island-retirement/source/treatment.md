# Deca-Form: Island Retirement

## Logline

After a supernatural storm destroys his cruise ship, Deca-Form must stop treating survival as a rescue mission and use his alien transformations to build a new life on an empty island.

## Central Conflict

Deca-Form wants to get back to civilization, but every attempt reminds him that his belt has limits: ten minutes of power, then a recharge. The real threat becomes panic, isolation, and the fear of being stranded with nobody to save him.

## Theme

Sometimes rescue is not a place you reach. Sometimes it is the moment you stop waiting and save yourself.

## Emotional Arc

Deca-Form begins as a hero whose alien belt makes him look unstoppable, but his anxiety has been climbing for a long time. He finally takes a cruise because he needs distance from heroic pressure. The shipwreck strips away certainty. He panics, searches, fails, and almost spirals. He regains control by narrowing the problem: breathe, survive, build, connect. By the end, he transforms the island from a sentence into a chosen home.

## Ending

The final page shows Deca-Form and his clones living in a handmade island settlement of glowing vine homes, garden terraces, beach roads, and alien-built lookout towers. The visual consequence of his choice is a complete new community where there had been only fear.
