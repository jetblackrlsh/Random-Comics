# Page Script

Every page is a full realistic candid photo-comic page in 4:5 portrait aspect ratio. All readable story text must be generated directly inside rectangular caption boxes in the page art. Use caption boxes only; no speech bubbles, no thought bubbles, no floating subtitles, no blank text boxes, no later text overlay.

## Page 1: Cover

- Story role: Present Jet, the anti-evolved war, the giant robot, Bronze Bear, and the first eruption of Lucid Light.
- Layout: Dynamic cover composition with one dominant night action image and two small inset panels.
- Caption/title text to generate in the page art: Title text: "SPARK OF LUCID LIGHT". Small caption box: "Jet Black dreamed of power. Then the war came through his ceiling."
- Visual beats: A giant adaptive capture robot holds Jet Black high above a shattered apartment roof. Jet matches the attached reference: Black man with long dreadlocks, full beard, black hoodie with white trim, dark shirt, black pants, white sneakers, crossbody bag. Purple cosmic Lucid Light begins to glow around his hands and chest. A black stealth jet cuts across the moonlit sky. Bronze Bear, a bronze-metal-skinned rescuer with sharp bear claws and a red-circle R insignia, drops toward the robot's face.

## Page 2: The Spark

- Story role: Establish the world after the Spark and Jet's belief that he remained ordinary.
- Layout: Five-panel page: world event, evolved powers, fearful crowds, drones, Jet alone.
- Caption boxes: "The Spark changed the world overnight." "Some people evolved." "Most people did not." "Fear became policy." "I thought I had been left behind."
- Visual beats: Purple aurora-like energy sweeps over Earth in a news-like panel; ordinary people reveal different powers in chaotic streets; fearful human crowds and armored police lines face evolved protestors; adaptive drones patrol city skylines; Jet watches from his apartment window in the same black outfit, guilty and powerless.

## Page 3: Dreams Become Narrative

- Story role: Show Jet's secret change: Lucid Light exists in coherent dreams before it appears awake.
- Layout: Four-panel page with waking life on top and dream imagery below.
- Caption boxes: "My waking life stayed ordinary." "But my dreams stopped being random." "Every night, Lucid Light answered me." "I could reshape anything except the morning."
- Visual beats: Jet at night in bed, the city outside tense with drones; his dream self floating in a cosmic purple Storyverse-like dreamscape; purple Lucid Light bends buildings and stars around his hands; Jet wakes with no visible power, staring at his normal hands in confusion.

## Page 4: Acquired Target

- Story role: The robot violently reveals that Jet is an evolved target.
- Layout: Five-panel page with a hard shift from sleep to disaster.
- Caption boxes: "One night, my body lifted before I woke." "The ceiling opened." "A hand closed around me." "The scan found what I could not." "EVOLVED TARGET ACQUIRED."
- Visual beats: Jet asleep in his apartment; a giant segmented robot hand crashes through the ceiling; Jet is lifted through debris in its fist; bright scanner eyes wash his shocked face in cold light; a rectangular machine-status caption panel announces target acquisition while Jet's expression reads disbelief.

## Page 5: Bronze Bear

- Story role: Introduce the resistance rescue and give Jet a chance to act.
- Layout: Four action panels plus one narrow overhead jet panel.
- Caption boxes: "Then the night split open again." "The resistance had found me first." "Bronze Bear dropped without a chute." "His claws bought me seconds." "Seconds were all I had."
- Visual beats: Matte black stealth jet streaks overhead; Bronze Bear drops from the belly hatch, bronze-metal skin shining, claws extended, red-circle R on chest; he slams into the robot's face and carves glowing cuts through armor; the robot's grip tightens around Jet; Jet closes his eyes and remembers the purple power from his dreams.

## Page 6: Lucid Light

- Story role: Jet manifests Lucid Light in the waking world and breaks free.
- Layout: Six-panel page building to one large purple shockwave panel.
- Caption boxes: "I stopped pulling against metal." "I reached for the dream." "Lucid Light reached back." "The hand shattered." "Then gravity made its claim."
- Visual beats: Jet takes a breath inside the robot's fist; purple starfield light blooms from his chest, eyes, and hands; a violet shockwave blows apart the robot's fingers; robot fragments tumble through dust; Jet falls from high above the apartment building, Lucid Light trailing from him like a comet.

## Page 7: Metal Meteor

- Story role: Bronze Bear saves Jet and moves him toward the decisive choice.
- Layout: Five-panel page: midair catch, impact, aftermath, swamp path, jet reveal.
- Caption boxes: "Bronze Bear left the wreckage behind." "He caught me before the ground did." "He hit like a meteor." "He stood up first." "The resistance was waiting in the swamp."
- Visual beats: Bronze Bear leaps from the robot's clawed-up face; he catches Jet mid-fall; the pair crash into a street or parking-lot crater with Bronze Bear taking the impact; Jet is protected but shaken; Bronze Bear leads Jet through reeds and cypress shadows toward a matte black stealth jet hidden beside dark water.

## Page 8: The Choice

- Story role: Resolve the one-shot with Jet choosing resistance over capture or false safety.
- Layout: Four-panel page ending on a strong final boarding image.
- Caption boxes: "Going with them meant running." "Staying meant a cage." "Either way, freedom had changed shape." "So I chose the people still fighting for choice."
- Visual beats: Jet stands at the open hatch of the black resistance jet, swamp water reflecting purple Lucid Light around his hands; Bronze Bear watches the damaged robot search in the far distance; Jet looks back toward his broken apartment and the life he is leaving; final wide panel shows Jet stepping into the jet with purple Lucid Light glowing softly, the red resistance mark visible as the hatch begins to close.
