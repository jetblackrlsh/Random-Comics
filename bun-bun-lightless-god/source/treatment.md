# Bun-Bun and the Lightless God

## Logline

When ash from a volcanic eruption starves a stranded Valordian crew of sunlight, their youngest explorer steals sacred ore from a cave god's shrine and survives only by turning her flight field into a desperate act of escape.

## Central Conflict

Bun-Bun needs ore to repair the damaged faster-than-light messaging system before her light-feeding people fade under the ash-darkened sky. The cave's humanoid dinosaur priest sees the same ore deposit as a living god, and he treats Bun-Bun's mining as sacrilege punishable by death.

## Theme

Survival pressure can make theft feel like strategy, but victory without consent leaves a debt that strength cannot settle.

## Emotional Arc

Bun-Bun begins as a brilliant but impulsive young explorer who believes necessity excuses action. The crash, weakness, and failed negotiation force her to confront the limits of power. She wins by improvising with her anti-gravity field, but the final image frames the rescue as morally incomplete: she saved her crew and still owes the world an apology.

## Ending

The ore repairs the messaging system and the Valordians call home, but Bun-Bun sits injured beside the doctor with glowing blood dimmed under the ash. Her final promise is not triumph but discipline: next time, diplomacy before taking.
