# Page Script

## Page 1: Cover

Story role: Establish the horror premise and the alley as a trap without revealing the story's supernatural twist.

Layout: One dominant vertical photo-comic cover image with two small inset panels near the bottom.

Caption/title text to generate in the page art: Title text: "THE DARK ABOVE". Small caption box: "After midnight, the alley waited."

Visual beats: The Vales stand outside the shabby theater at the mouth of a dark wet alley. Ethan runs a few steps ahead with his arms spread like a superhero while Adrian and Camille follow behind, concerned. The alley rises into deep blackness above them, with a fire escape and a flickering amber lamp suggesting something unseen overhead. Insets show the dying lamp and Adrian's hand holding an unlit black smartphone. Do not show flight, fangs, red eyes, monster faces, attacks, or the final chase.

## Page 2: After The Movie

Story role: Establish warmth, wealth, late night setting, and Ethan's superhero fantasy.

Layout: Four rectangular panels in a readable grid.

Caption boxes to generate in the page art:
- Top caption: "The movie ended after midnight."
- Small parent caption: "Stay close, Ethan."
- Small boy caption: "I'm flying!"

Visual beats: The Vales leave a shabby theater on a rough block. Ethan runs into the alley with arms spread. Adrian and Camille walk behind him, amused but uneasy. The overhead lamp flickers amber against wet brick.

## Page 3: The Lamp Goes Out

Story role: Turn playful distance into dread.

Layout: Five panels, with the final panel almost entirely black.

Caption boxes to generate in the page art:
- Top caption: "The light blinked once."
- Parent caption: "Ethan. Come back now."
- Final caption: "Then it did not return."

Visual beats: Ethan reaches the far end of the lit patch. Adrian's smile fades. Camille steps forward, hand out. The lamp flickers off, on, off. Darkness swallows the boy and the end of the alley.

## Page 4: Empty Light

Story role: Reveal the boy is missing and move the light source to the father's phone.

Layout: Four panels, increasingly tight and handheld.

Caption boxes to generate in the page art:
- Top caption: "Their voices came back as echoes."
- Father caption: "Ethan?"
- Mother caption: "No. No, where is he?"

Visual beats: Adrian turns on his black smartphone flashlight. The white beam scans dumpsters, brick, wet pavement. It reaches the end of the alley and finds no Ethan. Camille screams, hands over mouth.

## Page 5: From Above

Story role: Reveal Ethan flying and frame his new power as terrifying confusion.

Layout: Large vertical reveal panel with two smaller reaction panels.

Caption boxes to generate in the page art:
- Top caption: "The apology came from above."
- Boy caption: "I'm sorry."
- Boy caption: "I felt it before I could speak."

Visual beats: Adrian points the phone upward. Ethan floats near a fire escape, arms hanging, jacket rippling, stunned face, feet above the brick wall line. His parents stare up, frozen. The phone light makes harsh shadows under Ethan's face.

## Page 6: The Prick

Story role: Explain the sensation and set up Adrian's doctorly attempt to inspect the neck.

Layout: Six compact panels with tense closeups.

Caption boxes to generate in the page art:
- Father caption: "What do you feel?"
- Boy caption: "A prick in my neck. Then power."
- Mother caption: "This is not normal. Hospital. Now."
- Father caption: "I'm a doctor. Let me see."

Visual beats: Ethan descends slightly but stays airborne. He touches the side of his neck. Camille trembles. Adrian steadies her with one hand while keeping the phone raised. The alley is mostly black beyond the beam.

## Page 7: Show Me Yours

Story role: The boy weaponizes trust and transforms the father.

Layout: Three panels, including one violent motion panel and one aftermath panel.

Caption boxes to generate in the page art:
- Boy caption: "Show me yours first."
- Top caption: "He crossed the dark faster than fear."
- Final caption: "The phone hit the wall."

Visual beats: Ethan's expression turns blank. Adrian's eyes widen. Ethan flies down and tackles him to the wet pavement. The phone tumbles away, flashlight streaking across brick as Adrian screams in the dark.

## Page 8: Do Not Look Up

Story role: Resolve with Camille fleeing and the final monster reveal.

Layout: Four panels ending in a large final image.

Caption boxes to generate in the page art:
- Top caption: "Camille found the phone."
- Inner caption: "Do not look up."
- Final caption: "Behind her, the family followed."

Visual beats: Camille grabs the fallen phone with shaking hands. The beam shows an empty patch of alley where Adrian screamed. She begins to raise the light but cannot force herself to look up. She runs out of the alley and drops the phone. Final panel: the phone lies on the pavement, flashlight shining upward, illuminating Ethan and Adrian flying after her with glowing red eyes and fangs.
