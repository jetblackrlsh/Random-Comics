# Treatment

## Title

Anamorphis UAP

## Logline

A drained comic-book creator sees a blurry leaked military video of a luminous faceless being and chooses to turn that disputed evidence into art that helps people look for wonder again.

## Central Conflict

Eli Voss wants the world to feel magical, but every conversation around him reduces mystery to technical dismissal. The skepticism is not just intellectual pressure; it makes his daily life feel gray, lifeless, and hostile to inspiration.

## Theme

Mundanity becomes dangerous when it trains people to stop looking. Wonder is not a refusal of reality; it is a way of staying alive inside it.

## Ending

Eli publishes a new comic inspired by the Anamorphis footage. A reader looks up from the comic at dusk and notices a faint human-shaped light above the city. The ending does not prove the creature to everyone, but it proves that Eli's art can reopen the sky.

## Emotional Arc

Eli begins exhausted, colorless, and almost ashamed of his need for awe. The video gives him a private spark, but public skepticism nearly crushes it. He resolves the conflict by refusing to ask permission to feel wonder. By the end, his comic becomes an invitation for others to see the world as alive with possibility.

## Cast

- Eli Voss: late-20s comic-book creator, tired but observant, hungry for impossible beauty.
- Mara Keene: practical editor and friend, not cruel, but reflexively skeptical and protective of ordinary explanations.
- The Anamorphis UAP: luminous, faceless, humanoid, fast, flying, shapeshifting observer. It watches from above but never attacks or rescues.

## Page Count

Eight pages total: cover plus seven story pages.
