# Divine Repo

## Logline

Adam Frankenstein becomes God's repo man, reclaiming stolen Lucid Light from miracle thieves before the adaptive Immortal Curse finally ends the Frankenstein line.

## Premise

Adam Frankenstein is the latest heir to a family that survives by inventing new ways to kill the same immortal hunter. Victor Frankenstein created the Immortal Curse to prove mastery over life, then tried to destroy it when its protoplasmic body disgusted him. The Curse learned violence as its first language and has spent generations obeying one directive: kill Frankenstein.

Adam built Second Skin, a thought-reactive AI symbiote suit that manipulates matter at the atomic level. It was supposed to be the final answer. Instead, the Immortal Curse adapted to it. Adam's best weapon became another failed entry in the family ledger.

The only force Adam finds that may still erase the Curse is God's missing Lucid Light, a purple cosmic energy generated by the sleeping mind of a very rich man who tricked himself into an eternal lucid coma and forgot he was dreaming. God loaned most of that power to thieves, frauds, monsters, cult leaders, and would-be superheroes who used miracles to rewrite their lives, then refused to pay. God wants the power back because he loves money. Adam wants it back because it may be the only thing the Curse has not adapted to yet.

Divine Repo follows Adam and Second Skin as they collect one impossible debt at a time. Each debtor has turned borrowed divinity into a personal empire, and every recovered spark brings Adam closer to a weapon that might finally end the Frankenstein curse.

## Tone

Gothic science fiction meets cosmic debt collection. The series should feel grim, strange, practical, and darkly funny: ruined laboratories, predatory miracle economies, divine contracts written like loan paperwork, desperate chases through holy fraud, and a protagonist who treats apocalyptic theology like a job ticket.

## Recurring Visual Language

- Realistic candid photo-comic style with documentary phone-photo or handheld photojournalism framing.
- Adam's world uses damp basements, surgical work lights, handwritten family ledgers, improvised machines, black polymer armor, and purple divine energy intruding on grimy real spaces.
- Second Skin appears as a damaged black AI symbiote suit with silver liquid-metal seams, exposed luminous fault lines, and occasional matter-shaping tendrils.
- Lucid Light appears as purple cosmic energy with dreamlike dust, star-grain, glassy refraction, and bill-like rectangular light sigils.
- God's dream spaces combine billionaire excess, hospital equipment, casino lighting, gold fixtures, and unstable purple clouds.
- The Immortal Curse is wet, translucent, pale-gray protoplasm with half-formed human anatomy, bone-white nodules, black sensory pits, and weaponized limbs that look improvised from slime and scar tissue.
- Caption boxes only. No speech bubbles or thought bubbles.

## Continuity Notes

- Adam wants to end the Frankenstein family curse permanently, not merely survive one more attack.
- Second Skin can manipulate matter atom by atom in response to Adam's thoughts, but the Immortal Curse is now immune to being destroyed by that method.
- Second Skin speaks and reacts through suit displays, body-language changes, and Adam's captions. Its personality swings between forced optimism and devastated self-doubt after its failure against the Curse.
- God is physically a comatose former billionaire connected to medical equipment, but his lucid dream persona behaves like a confused, greedy dream god.
- Lucid Light is God's purple cosmic power. Most of it is missing because God loaned it to untrustworthy debtors.
- The Immortal Curse adapts to any method that kills or fully disables it, preventing the Frankenstein family from repeating successful tactics.
- Adam's divine repo work begins because God's missing Lucid Light may be the last untested way to destroy the Curse.
