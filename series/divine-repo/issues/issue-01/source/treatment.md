# Treatment

## Title

Past Due Miracle

## Issue Number

1

## Logline

After Second Skin fails against the Immortal Curse, Adam Frankenstein takes God's first repo job and reclaims stolen Lucid Light from a fake miracle preacher.

## Central Conflict

Adam needs a new way to destroy the Immortal Curse before it kills him, but the only promising weapon belongs to a confused God whose power has been stolen by debtors. Adam must decide whether to become a divine collector and take power back from a fraud without becoming another monster in the process.

## Theme

Survival without purpose becomes another curse; Adam needs a job that can aim his fear at something bigger than staying alive.

## Ending

Adam recovers the first small spark of Lucid Light from Brother Prosper Vale and uses it to repel the Immortal Curse for a few seconds. It is not enough to win, but it is enough to prove the premise. Adam accepts the Divine Ledger and begins the impossible collection route.

## Emotional Arc

Adam starts exhausted and nearly hopeless because his greatest invention failed. Second Skin starts ashamed and erratic. God's offer looks ridiculous, greedy, and insulting, but Adam slowly recognizes a practical path inside the absurdity. By the end, Adam and Second Skin are still afraid, but their fear has direction.

## Story Shape

Nine pages: cover, Who's Who character guide, Second Skin's failure, God's lucid coma offer, the first debtor's miracle service, Adam's infiltration, the repossession, the Immortal Curse interrupting the job, and Adam accepting the ledger after proving Lucid Light can still hurt the impossible.
