# Page Script

All pages are full realistic candid photo-comic pages in 4:5 portrait aspect ratio. Caption-box text must be generated directly inside the page art. Use caption boxes only, no speech bubbles, no thought bubbles, no floating subtitles, no blank text boxes, and no later text overlay.

## Page 1: Cover

- Story role: Introduce Adam as a gothic sci-fi collector caught between divine debt and inherited horror.
- Layout: Single dramatic cover image with a realistic photo-comic cover feel.
- Visual beats: Adam Frankenstein in damaged Second Skin stands in a rain-slick church parking lot at night, one hand holding the glowing Divine Ledger, the other wrapped in purple Lucid Light. Behind him, a neon megachurch sign flickers while the Immortal Curse rises from a drain like wet protoplasmic death. God's comatose face appears faintly in purple cloud reflections overhead.
- Caption/title text: Cover title text: "Divine Repo". Smaller issue title text: "Past Due Miracle".
- Prompt notes: Realistic gothic sci-fi photo-comic cover, readable title text generated inside the cover image.

## Page 2: Who's Who

- Story role: Give readers a clear guide to the four main series characters before the first story sequence.
- Layout: Clean in-universe dossier page with four equal character-profile panels arranged in a 2x2 grid, plus a small title bar.
- Caption/title text: Title text: "Who's Who". Each profile should show the character name and concise fields for motivation, goal, personality, and powers.
- Character text:
  - Adam Frankenstein. Motivation: End the family curse. Goal: Reclaim enough Lucid Light to destroy the Immortal Curse. Personality: Tired, stubborn, practical, protective. Powers: Mad science, survival planning, Second Skin interface.
  - Second Skin. Motivation: Prove it is not a failed weapon. Goal: Keep Adam alive and upgrade itself. Personality: Brilliant, anxious, overconfident, self-doubting. Powers: Thought reading, atomic matter manipulation, armor, tendrils.
  - God. Motivation: Get his stolen power and money back. Goal: Collect unpaid miracle debts. Personality: Confused, greedy, theatrical, lonely. Powers: Dream divinity, Lucid Light lending, miracle contracts.
  - Immortal Curse. Motivation: Kill Frankenstein. Goal: Adapt until Adam dies. Personality: Wordless, violent, relentless, wounded. Powers: Immortality, protoplasmic shapeshifting, adaptive immunity.
- Visual beats: Adam in damaged Second Skin, Second Skin close-up as living armor, God in purple hospital-casino dream light, and the Immortal Curse as a wet pale-gray hunter. Use reference-style portraits and small icon-like power details inside each dossier panel.
- Prompt notes: This page may be more text-forward than normal story pages, but text must still be generated directly inside the page art.

## Page 3: The Failed Answer

- Story role: Show that Adam's greatest invention no longer works.
- Layout: Four panels: bunker alarm, Second Skin attack, failed disassembly, Adam retreating.
- Caption boxes: "Every Frankenstein survived by inventing one new death." "Adam built his answer atom by atom." "The Curse learned it once." "Once was enough."
- Visual beats: Frankenstein Bunker Lab under red emergency light; Adam in Second Skin blasting the Immortal Curse with silver atomic tendrils; the Curse briefly breaks apart into mist and slime; the slime snaps back together around the tendrils and Adam staggers as Second Skin flashes amber error lights.

## Page 4: The Lender

- Story role: Introduce God and the missing Lucid Light problem.
- Layout: Five panels moving from Adam's research to God's dream office.
- Caption boxes: "The family records named one force the Curse had never met." "Lucid Light." "God still had it." "Mostly, God had receivables."
- Visual beats: Adam studies old Frankenstein ledgers and a glowing purple note; Second Skin projects an unstable scan; Adam enters God's Lucid Coma Penthouse through a purple dream fracture; God reclines in a hospital bed inside casino luxury; floating contracts show unpaid miracle balances.

## Page 5: First Account

- Story role: Establish the repo deal and first debtor.
- Layout: Four panels: God's pitch, Divine Ledger, Prosper's chapel exterior, Prosper on stage.
- Caption boxes: "God loaned miracles like money." "The borrowers took divinity and skipped the bill." "Adam needed a weapon." "God needed a collector."
- Visual beats: God pushes the heavy Divine Ledger toward Adam without any deformed or stray hands in the composition; pages flip to Brother Prosper Vale's overdue account with only normal visible hands; exterior of a converted shopping-center megachurch glowing purple and gold; Prosper Vale on stage making cash multiply in offering buckets while worshippers film on phones.

## Page 6: No Collateral Damage

- Story role: Show Adam's method and moral boundary during the job.
- Layout: Five panels of infiltration and crowd protection.
- Caption boxes: "Second Skin suggested seventeen violent solutions." "Adam chose the slow one." "The faithful had not stolen the light." "Their shepherd had."
- Visual beats: Adam and Second Skin slip through service corridors behind the chapel; Second Skin tendrils quietly disable locks and cameras; Prosper's miracle surge shakes the room; Adam creates black-silver shield ribs over the crowd as falling lights freeze midair; Prosper notices Adam near the glowing altar machine.

## Page 7: Repossession

- Story role: Pay off the repo premise by taking back the stolen miracle.
- Layout: Six quick panels with a clear collection sequence.
- Caption boxes: "The contract did not care about applause." "The balance was past due." "Adam touched the altar." "The miracle came loose screaming."
- Visual beats: Prosper floods the stage with purple Lucid Light; Adam plants one scarred hand on the altar donation machine; Second Skin forms a precise repo clamp around the light instead of around Prosper; purple energy unspools from cash, fake stained glass, and Prosper's glowing hands; the crowd sees the fraud as the miracle drains into a small vial of starlight.

## Page 8: Collection Dispute

- Story role: Bring back the Immortal Curse and prove Lucid Light can affect it.
- Layout: Five action panels.
- Caption boxes: "The Curse found him before the receipt printed." "Second Skin could not kill it." "The recovered spark could still surprise it." "Surprise was not victory, but it was time."
- Visual beats: The Immortal Curse crashes through the chapel wall in wet gray pieces; Second Skin's silver tendrils slice through it and fail again; Adam breaks the vial, releasing a sharp purple flash; the Lucid Light burns dreamlike cracks across the Curse's body; Adam shields civilians as the blinded Curse recoils into the rain.

## Page 9: The Ledger Opens

- Story role: Resolve issue 1 and launch the series mission.
- Layout: Four panels ending on a future-facing splash.
- Caption boxes: "One spark bought seconds." "Seconds were more than Adam had yesterday." "God opened the rest of the book." "Adam Frankenstein took the route."
- Visual beats: Adam returns to the bunker with torn Second Skin armor and the tiny recovered Lucid Light spark sealed in a machine; Second Skin's amber error lights soften to blue; God appears on a cracked monitor beside the Divine Ledger; final wide splash of ledger pages filling the bunker with hundreds of purple debtor names while the Immortal Curse watches from the rainy dark outside.
