Series Premise:
Dream Caster follows Bob Carlson, a tired local access technician who believes public broadcasting is sacred because it gives ordinary people the power to bring their ideas, voices, and imagined worlds to life.

After a supernatural event connects Earth's broadcast signals to the Storyverse, Bob becomes the wielder of a futuristic TV remote glowing with purple cosmic energy. The remote channels Lucid Light, cosmic purple dream energy that empowers dreamers. Through it, Bob can tune into the Storyverse and cast different story-forms into reality, temporarily transforming into heroes, creatures, and genre archetypes from fictional worlds that were forgotten, abandoned, canceled, or never created.

The Storyverse is a collection of cosmic bubble universes within the Dream Realm. Each bubble universe contains a possible story. Every possible story exists somewhere in the Storyverse.

Across the Storyverse, a war is being fought between the Dream Guardians and the Nightmares. The Dream Guardians protect imagination, creativity, heroic possibility, and living stories. The Nightmares corrupt stories with fear, despair, static, and destructive imagination, turning dreams into monsters and twisting entire bubble universes into weapons.

As Dream Caster, Bob defends Earth and the Dream Realm from Nightmare incursions, rogue channels, corrupted characters, lost-media monsters, and broken bubble universes leaking into reality. His mission is not only to fight evil, but to give forgotten dreams a signal.

At its core, Dream Caster is a superhero series about imagination as a living force, broadcasting as a magical act, and the idea that every story, even the ones abandoned before anyone saw them, still deserves a chance to shine.

Issue 1 Title:
Channel Zero

Issue 1 Premise:
Bob Carlson was a tired local access technician. He worked long hours supporting public broadcast television because he believed that the public deserved the power to bring show ideas to life. He gets a call in the middle of the night to investigate the sudden appearance of a rogue channel being broadcast by the station. A powerful thunderstorm rages overhead as he drives to the station. He enters the building at the stroke of midnight just as lightning strikes the station's massive satellite dish. The entire building surges with electricity. Bob's body pulses with lightning as his eyes fill with TV static. Inside his mind he sees flashes of cancelled shows that were never aired floating inside bubble universes: a classic rubber hose cartoon, an anime about a cosmic hero, and a puppet show about a robot. He falls to his knees as a futuristic TV remote materializes in his hand, glowing with purple cosmic energy.

Explosion and chaos draw his attention. He races outside and sees a kaiju covered in glitching TV static rampaging through the city. Bob feels a connection with the beast, a supernatural bond telling him they are connected to the same power but on opposite sides of the spectrum. The lightning and storm were consequences of a war raging across the Storyverse between the Dream Guardians and the Nightmares. Now that war has come to Earth, and Bob must fight for good using Lucid Light from the remote.

Bob instinctually activates the remote and transforms into the anime cosmic hero. He flies into the sky and strikes the monster with a starlight punch, but the monster retaliates with laser breath that launches him through buildings. Bob activates the remote again and transforms into a giant robot puppet. He grapples with the kaiju, but the kaiju bites through the strings keeping the puppet form upright. Bob collapses and turns back to normal as the kaiju raises its foot to stomp him. Desperate, Bob activates the remote again. In a burst of bright light, Bob as a cartoonish rubber spring launches the kaiju off him and into outer space. The city is safe for now, but Bob feels deep within his soul that this was only the first battle of many. He had always believed in the power of the broadcast, and now that power surges within him.
