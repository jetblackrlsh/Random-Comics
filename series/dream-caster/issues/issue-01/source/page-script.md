# Page Script

All pages are full realistic candid photo-comic pages in 4:5 portrait aspect ratio. Caption-box text must be generated directly inside the page art. Use caption boxes only, no speech bubbles, no thought bubbles, no floating subtitles, no blank text boxes, and no later text overlay.

## Page 1: Cover

- Story role: Introduce Bob as a humble broadcast worker becoming Dream Caster under a cosmic rogue signal.
- Layout: Single dramatic cover image with a documentary storm-photo feel.
- Caption/title text: Cover title text: "Dream Caster". Smaller issue title text: "Channel Zero".
- Visual beats: Bob Carlson stands in a rain-slick station parking lot beneath the huge rooftop satellite dish. The futuristic remote glows purple in his right hand. His eyes flicker with TV static. Behind him, translucent Storyverse bubbles and a looming Nightmare Static Kaiju silhouette break through the storm clouds.
- Prompt notes: Realistic superhero photo-comic cover, not painted poster art; readable title text generated directly inside the cover image.

## Page 2: The Public Signal

- Story role: Establish Bob's ordinary life, values, exhaustion, and the midnight call.
- Layout: Four panels: control room, community monitor wall, Bob taking the call, Bob driving through rain.
- Caption boxes: "Bob Carlson kept the signal alive." "He believed ordinary people deserved airtime." "At 11:47 p.m., the station called about a rogue channel." "Bob drove back through a storm that felt like it was waiting for him."
- Visual beats: Bob in the cramped local-access control room surrounded by monitors and cables; a monitor wall showing community cooking, local debate, and amateur puppet footage; Bob answering his phone with tired concern; Bob's old car crossing rain-slick streets toward the station.

## Page 3: Midnight At The Dish

- Story role: Bring Bob to the station and trigger the supernatural broadcast event.
- Layout: Five panels with escalating storm energy.
- Caption boxes: "The rogue channel had no source." "The clock hit midnight." "Lightning found the dish." "The station became a door."
- Visual beats: Bob unlocks the station side door under heavy rain; empty hallway lit by flickering fluorescents; security monitor showing static labeled Channel Zero; lightning striking the satellite dish; electrical purple-white surge blasting through cable racks and monitors.

## Page 4: Every Possible Story

- Story role: Show the Storyverse vision and the remote choosing Bob.
- Layout: Four panels moving from body horror to cosmic wonder.
- Caption boxes: "Static filled his eyes." "Behind the signal, Bob saw worlds." "Canceled. Forgotten. Never made." "Then the remote found his hand."
- Visual beats: Bob convulsing on the control room floor with TV-static eyes; translucent bubble universes in his mind showing an original classic rubber hose cartoon character who is different from Bob's later spring form; more Storyverse bubbles show an anime cosmic hero world and a robot puppet stage; Bob kneeling and gasping as the Dream Caster remote materializes in purple Lucid Light.

## Page 5: The Static Beast

- Story role: Reveal the kaiju and Bob's bond to the same power on opposite sides.
- Layout: Four panels: explosion, street reveal, emotional connection, decision.
- Caption boxes: "The blast outside pulled him back to Earth." "A Nightmare had found the city." "Bob felt the same signal in its bones." "Opposite side. Same war."
- Visual beats: Bob racing through the station door into rain; the Nightmare Static Kaiju rampaging between buildings; close shot of Bob clutching the remote as purple Lucid Light answers the kaiju's black static; Bob raising the remote while citizens flee behind him.

## Page 6: Cosmic Hero Channel

- Story role: First transformation succeeds briefly, then the kaiju proves stronger.
- Layout: Five action panels.
- Caption boxes: "Bob pressed a button he did not understand." "The Storyverse answered with a hero." "For one bright second, he believed he could win." "Then the Nightmare breathed fire made of dead signal."
- Visual beats: Purple channel rings wrap around Bob; he becomes an anime-inspired cosmic hero with Bob's tired eyes and a blazing starlight aura; he flies upward and punches the kaiju with a starlight fist; the kaiju fires a laser-breath blast of static; Bob is launched through building walls in a shower of rain and debris.

## Page 7: Puppet Channel

- Story role: Bob tries another story-form but learns every channel has limits.
- Layout: Five panels with scale and failure.
- Caption boxes: "He changed the channel again." "This time the dream had strings." "The robot puppet was strong." "Fangs tore through wire and left the puppet limp."
- Visual beats: Bob rises from rubble and clicks the remote; he transforms into a giant robot puppet with wooden-mechanical limbs, dangling control strings, and purple Lucid Light joints; puppet Bob grapples the kaiju and pushes it back; the kaiju bites through the puppet form's glowing control strings; close-up of the monster's massive foot menacingly looming above human Bob in preparation to stomp.

## Page 8: The Forgotten Spring

- Story role: Resolve the fight through a strange abandoned cartoon form and commit Bob to the mission.
- Layout: Four panels ending in a quiet victory image.
- Caption boxes: "Bob had one breath left." "So he gave the weirdest dream a signal." "The city saw a monster fly." "Bob knew Channel Zero was only the beginning."
- Visual beats: Bob on wet pavement as the kaiju's foot descends; Bob desperately clicks the remote and erupts into a black-and-white rubber hose cartoon spring glowing with purple Lucid Light; the spring launches the kaiju upward through storm clouds and into outer space trailing static; final panel shows Bob human again in the ruined street, remote glowing in his hand, sunrise-purple storm light over the city.
