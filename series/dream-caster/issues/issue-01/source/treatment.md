# Treatment

## Title

Channel Zero

## Issue Number

1

## Logline

A midnight lightning strike bonds local access technician Bob Carlson to a cosmic remote, forcing him to cast forgotten story-forms against a Nightmare static kaiju.

## Central Conflict

Bob believes broadcasting should give ordinary people a signal, but the first signal from the Storyverse arrives as a destructive Nightmare incursion. He must decide whether to run from the impossible or trust the Lucid Light now surging through him.

## Theme

Every abandoned story still deserves a chance to shine.

## Ending

Bob uses a ridiculous forgotten cartoon form to save the city, proving that even the strangest discarded idea can become heroic when someone gives it a signal.

## Emotional Arc

Bob begins exhausted and unnoticed, serving other people's dreams from behind the equipment rack. The rogue channel terrifies him, then reveals that the same power he reveres in public broadcasting exists as a living cosmic force. By the end, Bob is still scared, but he accepts that his belief in giving forgotten dreams a signal has become his responsibility.

## Story Shape

Eight pages: cover, Bob's public-access life and midnight call, the storm and rogue channel, lightning strike and Storyverse vision, Static Kaiju attack, anime cosmic hero battle, giant robot puppet failure, rubber hose spring victory and vow.
