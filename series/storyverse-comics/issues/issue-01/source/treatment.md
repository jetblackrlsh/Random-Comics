# Treatment

## Title

The Map That Feared the Ending

## Issue Number

1

## Logline

An anxious young mapmaker dreams into Inkfall Reef and must help a Dream Guardian trap a Nightmare that feeds on her fear of choosing the wrong ending.

## Central Conflict

Lena wants a perfect route before she acts, but the Eraser grows stronger every time she fears making the wrong choice. To save Inkfall Reef, she must stop treating uncertainty as failure and turn her fear into a tactic.

## Theme

A map is useful because it can be revised.

## Moral Message

You do not need a perfect plan before you begin. Courage is choosing a direction, paying attention, and changing course honestly when the path changes.

## Ending

Lena and Captain Quill trap the Eraser after Lena realizes her race for a perfect map is feeding it. She redraws their escape path into a loop that sends the Nightmare charging perfectly into erased space, stunning it long enough for Quill to seal it in an unfinished blank bubble. Inkfall Reef survives, but the final page shows other bubbles in the Cosmic Sea flickering with Nightmare wounds, making it clear this was one story in a much larger war.

## Emotional Arc

Lena begins terrified of being wrong, convinced every project and every choice must be perfect before anyone sees it. Inkfall Reef turns that private anxiety into a physical predator. Captain Quill cannot simply save her, because the Eraser tracks fear itself. Lena's turning point comes when she sees that chasing perfection has made both her and the Nightmare predictable, then revises the path instead of perfecting it. By the end, she still dislikes uncertainty, but she trusts herself to revise.

## Story Shape

Eight pages: cover, Lena's ordinary anxiety, arrival in the Storyverse and Cosmic Sea, Inkfall Reef and Captain Quill, the Eraser's first attack, map-path chase, revised-route trap, victory and series promise.
