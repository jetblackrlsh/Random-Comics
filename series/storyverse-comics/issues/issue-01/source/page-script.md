# Page Script

All pages are full realistic candid photo-comic pages in 4:5 portrait aspect ratio. Caption-box text must be generated directly inside the page art. Use caption boxes only, no speech bubbles, no thought bubbles, no floating subtitles, no blank text boxes, and no later text overlay.

## Page 1: Cover

- Story role: Introduce the series promise: a dreamer, a Dream Guardian, a fantastic world, and a Nightmare in a strategic battle.
- Layout: Single dramatic cover image with a realistic photo-comic cover feel.
- Caption/title text: Cover title text: "Storyverse Comics". Smaller issue title text: "The Map That Feared the Ending".
- Visual beats: Lena Ortiz stands on a folded paper-map bridge above Inkfall Reef, clutching her glowing sketchbook. Captain Quill stands beside her in Lucid Light Conduit armor with the cartographer spear raised. Behind them the Eraser rises from an ink waterfall, while the Cosmic Sea and floating bubble universes fill the sky.
- Prompt notes: Realistic candid photo-comic cover, not painted poster art. Readable title text generated directly inside the cover image.

## Page 2: Too Many Wrong Turns

- Story role: Establish Lena's ordinary fear of imperfection.
- Layout: Four panels: bedroom desk, close-up of map project, Lena erasing too hard, falling asleep.
- Caption boxes: "Lena Ortiz could draw any road except the first one." "Every route had to be perfect before anyone saw it." "By midnight, the paper was almost gone." "Sleep found her with the eraser still in her hand."
- Visual beats: Lena in her small bedroom under rain-lit window; school map project covered in erased pencil marks; her anxious face as paper tears under the eraser; her head on the desk beside the sketchbook and lamp.

## Page 3: The Sea Where Stories Rise

- Story role: Reveal the Storyverse and Cosmic Sea.
- Layout: Four panels moving from ordinary room to cosmic scale.
- Caption boxes: "She woke to waves under the stars." "The ocean was full of stories." "Worlds rose from the Cosmic Sea like bubbles." "One cracked open beneath her feet."
- Visual beats: Lena opens her eyes on a floating scrap of paper-map; vast Cosmic Sea with luminous aquatic beings below; bubble universes rising from violet water; a bubble-world of coral maps opens and pulls Lena toward Inkfall Reef.

## Page 4: Inkfall Reef

- Story role: Introduce the issue world, Captain Quill, Dream Guardians, and the larger war.
- Layout: Five panels: arrival, reef detail, Nightmare damage, rescue, Mister Sandman signal.
- Caption boxes: "Inkfall Reef charted every path a story might take." "Something was erasing the endings." "Captain Quill reached her before the fall did." "A signal whispered: Dream Guardians, hold the line."
- Visual beats: Lena tumbles onto a coral island with paper-map bridges; living cartography towers and ink waterfalls; a section of reef blanked into white nothing; Captain Quill catches Lena with a glowing map-line from his spear; brief Lucid Light image of Mister Sandman in the air.

## Page 5: The Eraser

- Story role: Reveal the Nightmare and its fear-sensing advantage.
- Layout: Five tense action panels.
- Caption boxes: "The Nightmare smelled unfinished work." "It wore the face of every mistake Lena feared." "Quill struck first." "The monster followed fear faster than steel."
- Visual beats: The Eraser crawls from an ink waterfall as wet black paper and eraser dust; its faceless head splits into false teacher, parent, and classmate faces; Captain Quill attacks with spear-drawn purple map-lines; red fear trails leak from Lena and the Eraser dodges toward them; the monster tears a bridge away beneath them.

## Page 6: The Map Must Be Perfect

- Story role: Turn the fight into a clear chase and show Lena's perfectionism actively endangering them.
- Layout: Five panels with forward motion down a map-path.
- Caption boxes: "The path became paper under their feet." "Behind them, the Nightmare erased every step." "Quill called, \"Map us to safety!\"" "Lena froze. \"It has to be perfect.\""
- Visual beats: Lena and Captain Quill sprint down a narrow path made of overlapping paper maps; the Eraser charges behind them, dragging ink claws that turn the path into blank white nothing; Quill glances back while holding his cartographer spear and tells Lena they need a map to safety; Lena clutches her glowing sketchbook, terrified because she believes any escape map must be flawless; the erased void closes in behind them.

## Page 7: The Loop

- Story role: Strategic turning point: Lena realizes the Nightmare is empowered by her perfectionism, then uses the chase pattern against it.
- Layout: Six quick tactical panels in reading order.
- Caption boxes: "Her need for perfection was feeding the nightmare." "Lena revised the path to loop back into the erased void of doom." "She pulled Quill aside." "The Eraser ran straight into what was gone."
- Visual beats: Quill warns Lena while they keep running, pointing to the Nightmare swelling with red fear trails from Lena's panic; Lena notices the Eraser is following the map-path with perfect precision instead of thinking; Lena redraws the path ahead so it loops back into the already-erased blank space; Lena grabs Quill and dives with him sideways off the path onto a small coral ledge; the Eraser races straight ahead along the loop and slams into the erased void; final panel shows the Nightmare stunned, half-suspended in blank white nothing with purple Lucid Light crackling around it.

## Page 8: A Map Can Change

- Story role: Resolve the conflict and promise the anthology's future.
- Layout: Four panels ending in a wide series-promise image.
- Caption boxes: "The unfinished route led exactly where it needed to." "The Eraser swallowed its own trap." "Inkfall Reef kept its endings." "Across the Storyverse, other worlds still called."
- Visual beats: Lena and Quill emerge behind the Eraser near a blank bubble; Quill seals the Nightmare inside with a purple map-line knot; Lena wakes at her desk and redraws her torn school map with confident revision marks; final panel shows the Cosmic Sea with many bubble universes, some bright and some wounded by Nightmare shadows.
