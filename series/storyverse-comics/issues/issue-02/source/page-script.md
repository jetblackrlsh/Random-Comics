# Page Script

All pages are full realistic candid photo-comic pages in 4:5 portrait aspect ratio. Caption-box text must be generated directly inside the page art. Use caption boxes only, no speech bubbles, no thought bubbles, no floating subtitles, no blank text boxes, and no later text overlay.

## Page 1: Cover

- Story role: Introduce Thomas, Sentinel Jax, the laser-drone crisis, and the issue's lesson about control.
- Layout: Single dramatic cover image with a realistic photo-comic cover feel.
- Caption/title text: Cover title text: "Storyverse Comics". Smaller issue title text: "Trigger Discipline".
- Visual beats: Thomas Jerry crouches behind a translucent purple Lucid Light barricade, tense but focused. Sentinel Jax leans from cover with the Lucid Light laser pistol cooling in his cybernetic hand. Above Neon Meridian, black-and-red infected laser drones descend through clean utopian towers.
- Prompt notes: Realistic candid photo-comic cover, not painted poster art. Readable title text generated directly inside the cover image.

## Page 2: Open Target

- Story role: Establish Thomas's ordinary flaw in the laser tag arena.
- Layout: Five panels: arena rush, opponents tagging him, overheated blaster, scoreboard loss, embarrassed walk-off.
- Caption boxes: "Thomas Jerry loved the action." "He ran straight at it." "He held the trigger until the blaster quit." "The scoreboard did not admire courage without cover."
- Visual beats: Thomas in red-and-black laser tag vest charges through neon maze walls; opposing players tag him from side angles; his plastic blaster flashes a heat warning and shuts down; scoreboard shows his team losing without needing readable numbers; Thomas lowers his head and walks toward the locker room.

## Page 3: Between Matches

- Story role: Move Thomas from disappointment into the Storyverse dream.
- Layout: Four panels: locker room, exhausted Thomas, Lucid Light glow, Cosmic Sea transition.
- Caption boxes: "Between matches, Thomas found the quietest bench." "He promised himself he would rush harder next time." "Sleep answered with purple light." "The Storyverse opened under him."
- Visual beats: Thomas sits alone in a realistic arena locker room with laser tag gear hooks and dim colored lights; he naps on a bench with his vest partly unbuckled; purple Lucid Light leaks from the blaster on the floor; his sleeping body dissolves into the Cosmic Sea and floating story bubbles.

## Page 4: Neon Meridian

- Story role: Introduce the issue's dream world as a utopian laser city before the crisis.
- Layout: Five panels: arrival, city marvels, civic laser technology, first glitch, drones activating.
- Caption boxes: "He woke in Neon Meridian." "Here, lasers healed, carried, grew, and cleaned." "Then red code crossed the sky." "Every helpful beam learned to hunt."
- Visual beats: Thomas stands in a bright futuristic plaza of glass towers and public gardens; laser-powered transit glides overhead; gentle laser medical kiosks and irrigation arrays help civilians; red glitch patterns spread across city laser projectors; black-and-red drones unfold from service stations and target frightened people.

## Page 5: Alley Save

- Story role: Escalate danger and introduce Sentinel Jax as the tactical Dream Guardian.
- Layout: Five action panels ending in the alley.
- Caption boxes: "Thomas ran toward the blast." "The drone saw an open target." "Sentinel Jax moved first." "Cover first. Questions second."
- Visual beats: Thomas instinctively sprints into the plaza toward a drone; a laser drone charges a red beam at his chest; Sentinel Jax tackles Thomas sideways just before the blast hits; both crash into a narrow alley lit by violet reflections; Jax pins Thomas down behind a wall while drones scan past the alley entrance.

## Page 6: Not Enough Gun

- Story role: Thomas uses Lucid Dreamer power, but misunderstands what kind of help is needed.
- Layout: Five panels: Jax explains, Thomas concentrates, pistol forms, Thomas argues, Jax points outward.
- Caption boxes: "Jax said, \"Make me a weapon.\"" "Thomas focused on winning fast." "Lucid Light became a pistol." "Jax said, \"A pistol is not a plan.\"" "Outside, someone proved it."
- Visual beats: Sentinel Jax gestures to Thomas's hands as purple Lucid Light gathers; Thomas squeezes his eyes shut and concentrates; a sleek purple Lucid Light laser pistol forms in Jax's cybernetic hand; Thomas looks confused and urges Jax toward the street; Jax silently points toward a futuristic officer outside the alley.

## Page 7: The Same Mistake

- Story role: Show Thomas the consequence of reckless rushing and constant fire.
- Layout: Six quick tactical panels.
- Caption boxes: "The officer charged straight ahead." "He held the trigger down." "The pistol overheated." "The drone did not." "Thomas finally recognized the mistake." "It was his."
- Visual beats: Futuristic officer in clean city armor rushes directly at the drone swarm; he fires nonstop from a laser pistol; heat warnings flare and smoke curls from the barrel; the pistol dies in his hand; a drone knocks him down with a red blast; Thomas watches from the alley with shocked recognition while Jax remains still behind cover.

## Page 8: Cooldown

- Story role: Resolve the conflict through Thomas's active choice to create cover and let Jax use disciplined timing.
- Layout: Six panels ending with Thomas waking ready for the next match.
- Caption boxes: "Thomas stopped asking for a bigger blast." "He made cover." "Jax fired once, then waited." "Shot by shot, the sky cleared." "When Thomas woke, the next match was waiting." "This time, so was he."
- Visual beats: Thomas summons translucent purple Lucid Light barricades across the alley mouth and plaza corners; Sentinel Jax ducks behind cover with the laser pistol; Jax fires one precise purple shot and lowers the pistol as cooling light fades; drones fall one at a time while civilians escape; Neon Meridian's clean lasers return to blue-white healing beams; Thomas wakes in the locker room, stands up, and tightens his laser tag vest with calm focus.
