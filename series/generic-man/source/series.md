# Generic Man

## Logline

In Super World, where everyone has force projection and almost everyone has flashy extra powers called Hacks, the world's greatest force projection user is dismissed as ordinary because he has no Hacks at all.

## Premise

Generic Man follows Genny Manson, the only known person in Super World born without any Hacks. He still has the universal baseline ability of force projection: projecting invisible fields of force from his body to fly, shield himself, strike, lift objects, and move through the world with impossible pressure. Because everyone has force projection, society treats it as a basic utility power instead of a heroic specialty.

Genny becomes the most powerful force projection user in the world by training the ignored skill harder than anyone else. The Hero Association barely notices. Its dispatch system is built around matching heroes' Hacks to villains' Hacks, and it rarely values mastery when a more specialized countermeasure is available. Genny is genuinely kind and wants to help because he knows what it feels like when nobody helps.

The series asks what makes someone special, whether being the best at something matters when no one values that thing, and whether a hero is defined by how many tools they have or how well they use the one tool they were given.

## Setting

Super World is a bubble universe inside the greater Storyverse, a collection of bubble universes within the Dream Realm where every possible story exists somewhere. Super World is a superpowered society where everyone possesses force projection and almost everyone possesses additional abilities called Hacks.

Force projection is common enough to be culturally invisible. Hacks are treated as identity, status, career qualification, fashion category, and combat taxonomy. People are easily identified by their Hacks signatures, so secret identities are mostly pointless. Heroes and villains usually act under civilian identities.

The Hero Association governs hero deployment and public safety. Its systems prioritize whether a hero has the correct Hacks to counter a current villain. Traditional labor has largely disappeared because humanoid robots handle most routine work. People usually spend their time in leisure, Hero Association service, or open rebellion against the status quo as villains.

Fashion is dominated by Hacks suits: colorful armored suits custom built to regulate and express each person's Hacks. Genny's future vigilante suit rejects that culture with a completely blank white bodysuit, a white cape, and a fully obscuring faceless mask.

## Tone

Earnest superhero underdog drama with satirical bureaucracy and bright superpowered spectacle. The series should feel clean, public, and strangely lonely: a world full of colorful super suits, signature powers, automated comfort, and official hero systems that still fail to recognize quiet excellence.

## Recurring Visual Language

- Realistic candid photo-comic style with documentary phone-photo or handheld photojournalism framing.
- Super World cities use polished civic towers, clean public plazas, colorful Hacks suits, helpful humanoid robots, floating drones, soft force-field distortions, and Hero Association signage.
- Force projection is mostly invisible but can be shown through air-pressure ripples, lensing, dust rings, impact halos, and objects held by no visible hand.
- Hacks signatures are unique visible effects: colored fire, glassy light, magnetic distortion, plant growth, particle swarms, hardlight glyphs, or other personalized phenomena.
- Genny's visual contrast is restraint: simple clothes as a child, later a completely blank white suit and cape with no logo, no color accents, and no decorative identity marker.
- Caption boxes only. No speech bubbles or thought bubbles.

## Continuity Notes

- Genny Manson is the only known person on Super World with no Hacks beyond force projection.
- Genny has not received formal Super Academy training because mandatory Hacks exams excluded him.
- Genny's skill comes from self-directed practice, observation, and imitation of helper robots, construction robots, and drones.
- The Hero Association values counter-Hacks dispatch logic over broad mastery.
- Everyone has force projection, but most people do not train it seriously because it is considered generic.
- Genny's kindness is not naivety. It is a deliberate response to a childhood where adults and systems abandoned him.
- Generic Man's white vigilante suit should remain blank and faceless, turning the insult "generic" into a chosen symbol.
