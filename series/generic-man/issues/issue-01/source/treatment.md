# Treatment

## Title

Nothing Special

## Issue Number

1

## Logline

Rejected at birth for having no Hacks, Genny Manson turns the world's most ignored power into the first proof that he can become a hero.

## Central Conflict

Super World measures worth through Hacks. Genny has none. He must decide whether that judgment defines him or whether disciplined use of force projection can become a heroic path even when the world calls it generic.

## Theme

Mastery matters even when status refuses to recognize it.

## Ending

After defending himself from bullies with force projection, Genny chooses to treat "generic" as a badge instead of a wound. He imagines the blank white suit of the hero he will become: someone with no special decoration, no accepted category, and no excuse to stop helping.

## Emotional Arc

Genny moves from innocent rejection to lonely observation, then deliberate training, then the first moment of self-definition. The world does not change for him in this issue, but Genny changes his relationship to the world's insult.

## Story Shape

Eight pages: cover, birth scan, abandonment and foster drift, bullying and robot inspiration, force-field training, strength and flight training, confrontation with the bullies, and Genny accepting the name Generic Man.
