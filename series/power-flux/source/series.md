# Power Flux

## Logline

A selfish dreamer resurrected by an angel of Limbo must save Earth from alien criminals with unstable powers that change without warning, or lose his soul to Hell.

## Premise

Power Flux follows Rann Dominique, a man who hated ordinary life so much that he wished on a falling star for superpowers. The falling star was not a blessing. It was an alien prison rock filled with the galaxy's worst exiles, and its crash killed Rann while scattering the prisoners across Earth.

Rann's spirit is intercepted in Limbo by Dark Angel, a grim angel who carries both heavenly and hellish traits. Dark Angel judges Rann's wish as selfish enough for damnation, but a higher mercy blocks Rann from Hell. Rann is sent back to life with unstable powers that randomly shift from moment to moment, a dark glittering halo, and extradimensional armor marked by glowing question marks.

The series is about improvising in impossible fights, but its deeper test is moral. Rann cannot earn salvation by protecting his own soul alone. He must learn to fight because other people matter, even when the universe gives him no control over what power comes next.

## Tone

High-stakes redemption superhero fantasy with cosmic horror edges, strange alien fugitives, dark spiritual comedy, and kinetic improvisational action. The emotional tone should keep Rann frightened, amazed, defensive, and gradually humbled.

## Recurring Visual Language

- Realistic candid photo-comic style with documentary phone-photo or handheld photojournalism framing.
- Rann's powers visibly change between panels: flight contrails, slime body distortion, fire breath, superstrength impacts, sudden energy fields, strange limbs, or other photoreal VFX.
- Rann's armor is dark extradimensional metal with shifting glowing question marks. The question marks change color whenever a new power manifests.
- Rann's dark glittering halo floats above his short black curly afro after resurrection.
- Dark Angel appears through Limbo gateways, never fully comfortable in Earthly space.
- Limbo imagery combines pale fog, black-gold cracks, suspended doors, distant stairways, and gates that imply Heaven and Hell without showing them as simple places.
- Alien prison imagery should feel like real crashed space debris: cracked stone-metal, containment glyphs, cryo cells, smoke, and jagged extradimensional light.
- Caption boxes only. No speech bubbles or thought bubbles.

## Continuity Notes

- Rann Dominique caused the prison crash by selfishly wishing for superpowers on what he thought was a shooting star.
- The alien prison rock exploded on Earth and released its prisoners worldwide.
- Rann died in the crash, entered Limbo, was denied immediate passage into Hell, and returned to life under a redemption bargain.
- Rann's powers are undefined and randomly shifting. He cannot choose them at will at the start of the series.
- Rann must save Earth from the fugitives he released. His salvation depends on becoming selfless, not merely surviving.
- Dark Angel guides Rann from Limbo through dimensional gateways but can only interfere so much in Earthly affairs.
- Dark Angel ultimately must send Rann either to Heaven or Hell.
- Rann's post-resurrection visual identity is the dark glittering halo, the question-mark armor, and rapidly changing power effects.
