# Page Script

All pages are full realistic candid photo-comic pages in 4:5 portrait aspect ratio. Caption-box text must be generated directly inside the page art. Use caption boxes only, no speech bubbles, no thought bubbles, no floating subtitles, no blank text boxes, and no later text overlay.

## Page 1: Cover

- Story role: Introduce Rann's new identity, unstable powers, Dark Angel, and the alien prison catastrophe.
- Layout: Single dramatic cover image with documentary superhero cover energy.
- Visual beats: Rann in Flux Armor stands in the smoking crash field with a dark glittering halo above his short curly afro. Glowing question marks on his armor flare in several colors. One hand leaks fire, the other stretches into living slime, and his feet hover slightly above cracked earth. Dark Angel looms in a black-gold Limbo gateway behind him. Alien prison fragments burn in the crater.
- Caption/title text: Cover title text: "Power Flux". Smaller issue title text: "The Selfish Wish".
- Prompt notes: Realistic superhero photo-comic cover, readable title text generated inside the cover image, no publisher logo.

## Page 2: Ordinary

- Story role: Establish Rann as restless, selfish, and hungry for a more exciting life.
- Layout: Four panels: cramped apartment, city sidewalk, neglected people nearby, Rann under the night sky.
- Caption boxes: "Rann Dominique hated ordinary life." "Every day felt too small for him." "He wanted wonder, danger, applause." "He never asked what anyone else needed."
- Visual beats: Rann bored in a messy apartment scrolling past crisis headlines; Rann walking through city life while ignoring a person asking for help; Rann watching emergency news with envy instead of concern; Rann alone in a grassy field at night as a bright streak crosses the sky.

## Page 3: Wishfall

- Story role: Show the selfish wish and reveal the falling star as a prison.
- Layout: Five panels with escalating scale.
- Caption boxes: "He saw a shooting star." "He could have wished for peace." "He could have wished for mercy." "Rann wished for power." "The star answered wrong."
- Visual beats: Rann smiling up at the streak; his hands clasped in selfish excitement; the star grows into a massive stone-metal object with blue-white cracks; close view of alien containment glyphs on its surface; final wide panel of the prison rock tearing through clouds toward Rann.

## Page 4: The Prison Opens

- Story role: Kill Rann and release the alien criminals.
- Layout: Six panels: impact, death flash, cracking prison, fugitives, global scatter, silence.
- Caption boxes: "It was not a star." "It was a prison." "Rann's wish broke the lock." "The worst things in the galaxy fell free." "Then Rann fell farther."
- Visual beats: The alien prison rock slams into the field in a blinding impact; Rann's silhouette thrown by the shockwave; prison cells split open in the crater; monstrous alien silhouettes escaping into smoke and light; streaks of escaped prisoners arcing across a map-like view of Earth; Rann's spirit rising from his body into pale fog.

## Page 5: Limbo

- Story role: Bring Rann before Dark Angel and state the moral charge.
- Layout: Four panels in the Limbo Threshold.
- Caption boxes: "Rann woke where endings wait." "Dark Angel had seen the wish." "Selfish. Small. Catastrophic." "He opened the gate Rann had earned."
- Visual beats: Rann's translucent spirit kneeling on fractured black stone; Dark Angel towering over him in pale fog; Dark Angel gesturing through a gateway that shows Earth covered in red warning points; a burning red gateway to Hell opens at Rann's back.

## Page 6: Mercy Blocks The Gate

- Story role: Show the invisible mercy that prevents immediate damnation and sets up the bargain.
- Layout: Five panels: shove, impact, failed gateway, annoyed relief, cosmic window.
- Caption boxes: "Dark Angel pushed." "Rann did not pass through." "Something unseen held him back." "Mercy, Dark Angel called it." "Mercy came with work."
- Visual beats: Dark Angel drives Rann toward the red gateway; Rann slams against an invisible force field before Hell; cracks of white light ripple around Rann; Dark Angel looks annoyed but relieved; a black-gold gateway opens to show alien fugitives landing in cities, deserts, and oceans.

## Page 7: Terms Of Power

- Story role: Explain Rann's unstable powers and make him accept the mission.
- Layout: Six quick panels showing power shifts in Limbo.
- Caption boxes: "Rann had asked for powers." "He had not asked which ones." "Flight became slime." "Slime became fire." "Fear said yes first." "Something better would have to follow."
- Visual beats: Dark Angel presents the Flux Armor; the armor forms over Rann's spirit; Rann suddenly flies upward in panic; he drops as his body becomes living slime; he coughs a jet of fire that lights his halo from below; Rann kneels in armor, terrified but agreeing while Dark Angel points toward Earth.

## Page 8: Back To Earth

- Story role: Resolve the origin and launch Rann's redemption journey.
- Layout: Four panels ending in a wide final image.
- Caption boxes: "Limbo threw him back." "The dead man breathed." "The question marks changed color." "Rann had wanted a miracle for himself. Now Earth needed one from him."
- Visual beats: Rann rockets out of a black-gold gateway toward the crash field; his body re-forms beside the crater in Flux Armor with a dark halo; his armor question marks flare as one hand burns and the other turns glassy; final wide panel of Rann standing amid alien wreckage while Dark Angel watches from a floating gateway and distant news helicopters search the night.
