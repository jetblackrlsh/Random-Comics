## Logline

A naive swamp-raised young man follows a demon-fed fairy tale across a medieval world to wake a god who does not exist, only to awaken a cosmic horror and force magic violently back into the world.

## Premise

Quest Series follows Wes, a bold and uneducated young adult from The Shit Water, a desolate swamp where subtle magic and old monster fears still linger. Wes grew up believing his parents' stories about Phil, a sleeping god who could fix the world's pain if someone brave enough found and woke him. After his parents die and his older sister Genevieve leaves for The City, Wes's fear and loneliness manifest as a personal demon that encourages his belief instead of challenging it.

Wes travels through wilderness, villages, and dangerous frontiers, gathering followers by promising that Phil will make the world safe and whole. His quest becomes a faith, then a burden, then a disaster. At the edge of reality, he leaps into The Hole and brushes against a higher-dimensional being that wanted only isolation. The being's anger awakens uncontrolled magic across the planet and threatens to destroy the world.

The series is about Wes being forced to grow up, put away childish beliefs, confront the harm caused by his certainty, and sever his connection to the cosmic horror before the world is consumed.

## Tone

Character-driven coming-of-age medieval fantasy with drama, adventure, grief, religious delusion, and escalating cosmic horror. The emotional center should stay with Wes and Genevieve: siblings shaped by the same childhood, split by different relationships to truth, memory, and survival.

## Recurring Visual Language

- Realistic candid photo-comic style with documentary phone-photo or handheld photojournalism framing.
- The Shit Water should look damp, poor, isolated, and half-haunted: mud, reeds, black water, decaying cabins, rough graves, lantern glow, mist, and subtle impossible ripples.
- Wes's belief in Phil should initially feel warm and storybook-like, but visually become harsher, cult-like, and dangerous as his followers grow.
- Wes's Demon appears as a fear beast thoughtform: half-seen in reflections, smoke, branches, mud, and candle shadows before taking clearer shape.
- The Hole should look like a natural place where reality fails: a bottomless black pit at the world's edge, windless dust, warped horizon, no visible bottom.
- Cosmic horror imagery should avoid simple monster design. Use impossible scale, wrong angles, starless negative space, huge partial forms, and reality-bending light.
- Magic returning should look uncontrolled and violent: cracks of luminous force through fields, storms bending backward, water rising, people glowing in fear, animals fleeing.
- Caption boxes only. No speech bubbles or thought bubbles.

## Continuity Notes

- Wes believes Phil is a sleeping god because his parents used that fairy tale to comfort him in The Shit Water.
- Genevieve knows the stories are not true, but delays telling Wes because the stories preserve his emotional bond to their parents.
- Genevieve leaves The Shit Water for The City with her husband, leaving Wes behind.
- Wes's Demon is born from Wes's fear and the swamp's subtle magic. It feeds on fear and hopes Wes's quest will end in despair.
- Wes gathers an adventuring party and, over years, becomes close to a cult leader by promising that Phil will fix the world.
- The Cosmic Horror is a higher-dimensional being that wants isolation. Wes's intrusion convinces it the world must be destroyed.
- When Wes contacts the Cosmic Horror through The Hole, uncontrolled magic awakens across the planet.
