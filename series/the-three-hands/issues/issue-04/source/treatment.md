# Treatment

## Title

The King's Three Hands

## Issue Number

4

## Logline

Kaine tests his kingdom's strongest defenders against hardlight Anamorphis simulations and learns that the right Three Hands must be knights who can harm light, keep choosing the fight, and still trust the king he once was.

## Central Conflict

Kaine needs three defenders for the Lucid Light within him, but his city has too many superhumans to choose from and too much recent pain for a simple appointment to feel legitimate. He narrows the choice through strategy rather than pride: existing knights first, light-affecting powers second, and personal trust after his past coercion above all.

## Theme

Power only becomes protection when it is paired with consent.

## Ending

Kaine promotes Blake Hollister, Buster Green, and Amani Rory from soldiers to the King's Three Hands. Blake becomes the Black Hand, Buster becomes the Green Hand, and Amani becomes the Red Hand. They accept the posts by choice and stand beside Kaine as the city's first true anti-Anamorphis defense.

## Emotional Arc

Kaine begins overwhelmed by the number of possible defenders and haunted by the knowledge that his old command structure was built on control. Each test shows him that raw power is not enough. By the end, he chooses three soldiers whose powers can threaten luminous invaders, whose identities still belong in battle, and whose willingness to trust him makes the appointment morally possible.

## Story Shape

Eight pages: cover, Kaine defining the impossible choice, the existing-knight criterion, the light-affecting criterion, the trust criterion, Blake's black-hole close-combat trial, Buster and Amani's contrasting trials, and the formal appointment of the Black Hand, Green Hand, and Red Hand.
