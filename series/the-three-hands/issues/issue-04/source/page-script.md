# Page Script

All pages are full realistic candid photo-comic pages in 4:5 portrait aspect ratio. Caption-box text must be generated directly inside the page art. Use caption boxes only, no speech bubbles, no thought bubbles, no floating subtitles, no blank text boxes, and no later text overlay.

## Page 1: Cover

- Story role: Introduce the issue as Kaine's strategic formation of the team.
- Layout: Single dramatic cover image with documentary superhero-photo framing.
- Caption/title text: Cover title text: "The Three Hands". Smaller issue title text: "The King's Three Hands".
- Visual beats: Kaine stands in the Royal Hardlight Simulation Room, crimson suit and purple cheetah-print cape lit by Lucid Light. Behind him, three candidate silhouettes resolve into Blake, a Black bald man in black armor; Buster, a Black man with spiky brown hair in green armor; and Amani in red armor. A translucent hardlight Anamorphis simulation glows in front of them, visibly artificial and weaker than the real thing.
- Prompt notes: Readable title text generated inside the cover image. Realistic candid photo-comic cover, not painted poster art.

## Page 2: Too Many Protectors

- Story role: Establish Kaine's impossible choice and his refusal to treat his people like tools again.
- Layout: Four panels: royal strategy table, city montage, candidate list, Kaine alone with Lucid Light.
- Caption boxes: "Kaine needed three defenders." "His city had hundreds of miracles." "Old Kaine would have commanded them." "The new king had to ask who still chose him."
- Visual beats: Kaine studies floating rosters in the palace with the island city visible below. Panels show superhuman citizens performing civilian roles and patrol roles across the tropical city. A roster overloads with colorful power icons. Kaine closes his hand around gentle purple Lucid Light, troubled rather than triumphant.

## Page 3: First Cut: Knights Only

- Story role: Explain why Kaine limits candidates to existing knights and does not pull citizens away from lives they choose.
- Layout: Five panels: civilian experts, former soldiers changing roles, knight line, Kaine marking the first criterion, a narrowed candidate field.
- Caption boxes: "The first cut was duty." "A healer belonged in a clinic." "A builder belonged where the city rose." "A former soldier could leave." "Only knights already carrying the burden stayed on the board."
- Visual beats: A superhuman healer restores a damaged market; a stone-skinned builder lifts palace debris; a former soldier turns in a helmet and embraces a family; current knights stand in colorful armor at attention by choice; Kaine moves non-knights out of the selection display without anger.

## Page 4: Second Cut: Can It Touch Light?

- Story role: Show why raw power does not matter against light-bodied Anamorphis unless the power can affect light.
- Layout: Six test panels inside the simulation room.
- Caption boxes: "The second cut was substance." "Some powers could shatter mountains." "Some could move oceans." "Against light, they passed through nothing." "The wrong miracle still missed." "Only light-touching power mattered."
- Visual beats: Hardlight Anamorphis simulations stand in a grid arena. A stone-powered knight punches through a translucent target without disrupting it. A storm knight's wind bends around the light body. A metalkinesis knight throws and bends metal shards that pass through the glow. A sonic-powered knight launches a visible pressure-wave attack that rattles the safety rails but passes through the simulated light body without damage. Kaine watches diagnostic panes mark the failures, including "Metalkinesis Trial: Fail", and writes the rule that only light-touching power matters.

## Page 5: Final Cut: Trust

- Story role: Make trust after Kaine's past coercion the most important criterion.
- Layout: Four panels with quieter emotional contrast.
- Caption boxes: "The final cut was not power." "He had stolen choices before." "Freedom did not make forgiveness automatic." "No hand could protect a king it did not trust."
- Visual beats: Knights remove old control insignia from armor; several soldiers walk away from the palace without being stopped; some civilians look grateful but guarded as Kaine passes; Blake, Buster, and Amani remain in the training chamber, each waiting by choice while Kaine looks ashamed and relieved.

## Page 6: Blake Hollister, The Black Hand

- Story role: Introduce Blake's identity, combat style, and anti-light suitability.
- Layout: Five panels: stance, close-combat exchange, black-hole reveal, restraint, Kaine's assessment.
- Caption boxes: "Blake Hollister measured himself in combat." "A soldier was not his cage." "It was his craft." "His black holes could bend light itself." "That made discipline more valuable than force."
- Visual beats: Blake, bald in a black battle suit, squares up bare-handed against a hardlight Anamorphis simulation. He slips past a luminous strike and counters with precise close combat. A tiny controlled black-hole distortion blooms near his fist and pulls the simulation's light off balance. He closes the singularity before it threatens the room. Kaine marks his name with solemn approval.

## Page 7: Green And Red

- Story role: Introduce Buster and Amani through contrasting trials.
- Layout: Six panels split between Buster's volatile control and Amani's lazy efficiency.
- Caption boxes: "Buster Green carried heat in his bones." "Combat gave the fire a shape." "His green light could meet theirs." "Amani Rory preferred the shortest workday." "His gauntlets turned laziness into speed." "Hardlight cut hardlight."
- Visual beats: Buster breathes through anger before entering the grid, green light blazing around his hands. He drives a green luminous punch through a simulated Anamorphis shield, then steadies himself with visible effort. Amani slouches near the wall, then snaps awake as his red gauntlets form a hardlight blade and hammer. He ends two simulations in a few efficient moves and immediately relaxes again.

## Page 8: The Three Hands

- Story role: Resolve the selection conflict with the team's appointment and mission.
- Layout: Four panels ending on a ceremonial group image.
- Caption boxes: "Kaine chose soldiers who still chose him." "Blake became the Black Hand." "Buster became the Green Hand." "Amani became the Red Hand." "Together, they would guard the king, the city, and the Lucid Light from the Anamorphis."
- Visual beats: Kaine faces the three candidates on the royal terrace above the tropical city. He promotes Blake, Buster, and Amani without using Lucid Light to compel them. Each accepts by choice: Blake solemn, Buster intense but controlled, Amani relaxed but alert. Final broad panel: the Three Hands stand beside Kaine while distant training-room hardlight Anamorphis silhouettes flicker behind them as a warning of the real war coming.
