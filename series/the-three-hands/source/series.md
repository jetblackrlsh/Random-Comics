# The Three Hands

## Logline

In a tropical island city inside the Storyverse, a redeemed king who once abused Lucid Light appoints three elite defenders to protect his people from Dark Light and the Anamorphis invaders hunting his power.

## Premise

The Storyverse is a multiverse of bubble universes where every possible story exists somewhere within the Dream Realm. Some worlds are fluid enough for Lucid Light to guide their events; others have already become rigid, and even Lucid Light cannot alter stories that are already written. One of those worlds is a tropical island city populated by superhuman citizens in colorful battle suits. Its ruler, King Kaine Chester, possesses Lucid Light: glittering purple cosmic energy capable of manipulating the Dream Realm with godlike precision when a story remains flexible enough to move.

Kaine once used Lucid Light to puppet his own people against their will. The city nearly rebelled before the Lucid Lord, a being made of Lucid Light, arrived with a futuristic crystal rainbow sword and a golden energy tether. The Lucid Lord's sword healed instead of harmed, and his tether cleansed the corruption from Kaine's soul. Kaine swore never to wield Lucid Light for control again.

The Lucid Lord warned Kaine that other powers would come for Lucid Light. The greatest threat is Dark Light, the corrupted ruler of the Anamorphis, a race of faceless luminous shapeshifters from beyond the island's world. Before reaching Kaine's city, the Lucid Lord tried to save his own rigid homeworld from Dark Light and failed, proving that Dark Light's corruption cannot simply be healed from inside that world's fixed story. Dark Light sends one Anamorphis soldier at a time through the Anamorphis Storyverse Gateway to study Kaine's defenses, learn from every defeat, and move closer to claiming Lucid Light for dominion over the Storyverse.

To keep his promise, Kaine creates the King's Three Hands: Blake Hollister, Buster Green, and Amani Rory, three defenders trusted to protect the city, the Dream Realm, and Lucid Light from extraterrestrial threats.

## Tone

Mythic superhero action with a redemption spine. The series should feel vivid, tropical, cosmic, and tactical: royal island politics, luminous dream-energy battles, colorful battle suits, and enemies who treat every fight as data for the next invasion.

## Recurring Visual Language

- Realistic candid photo-comic style with documentary phone-photo or handheld photojournalism framing.
- Tropical island city streets with bright ocean light, volcanic stone, palm trees, white towers, neon battle-suit colors, and royal purple Lucid Light reflecting on glass and water.
- Lucid Light appears as glittering purple cosmic energy: star-like motes, clean geometric ribbons, and lensing distortions in the air.
- Anamorphis energy bodies are faceless luminous humanoids with smooth silhouettes, no eyes or mouths, and impossible speed blur.
- Dark Light is rainbow-colored luminous energy shrouded by a crackling black corruptive aura.
- Caption boxes only. No speech bubbles or thought bubbles.

## Continuity Notes

- Kaine's vow is binding: after Issue #1, he must not use Lucid Light to control minds or bodies.
- Kaine may guard Lucid Light, speak about it, resist theft of it, and make political decisions around it, but he relies on the Three Hands for direct defense.
- The Lucid Lord has already tried and failed to cleanse Dark Light.
- Dark Light's corruption is part of his narrative and cannot be simply purified away.
- Lucid Light can manipulate the Dream Realm only where a story remains fluid enough to change; it cannot rewrite rigid stories that have already been written.
- Dark Light's homeworld is rigid, making Lucid Lord's power practically useless there and forcing him to warn other worlds instead of winning from within.
- The Anamorphis Storyverse Gateway expands the conflict beyond one world by letting Dark Light dispatch soldiers across bubble universes.
- The Anamorphis invasion strategy starts with single-soldier tests, each soldier revealing a different unique technique.
