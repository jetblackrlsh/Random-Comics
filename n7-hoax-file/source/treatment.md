# N-7: The Hoax File

## Logline

When Anonymous releases a buried 1994 contact file about N-7, a non-human feline humanoid recovered at the Nevada Test and Training Range, the world calls it a hoax. The leak argues that the hoax story was built to hide the only witness who told the truth.

## Central Conflict

Anonymous is trying to force the public to see a living person inside a declassified lie. The power structure has already chosen the word "hoax" because it is cheaper than explaining the patrol report, the medical exam, the transfer log, and the subject who spoke English while asking to be released.

## Theme

Calling evidence fake is easy when the subject has no voice. Truth requires looking at the person the file tried to reduce to a code.

## Emotional Arc

The comic starts as a cold file drop: folder scans, timestamps, and Anonymous framing. It shifts into the night of contact, then into medical processing, where N-7 becomes less mysterious and more vulnerable. By the end, the reader sees why the file was attacked as a hoax: not because it was weak, but because it was dangerous.

## Ending

Anonymous publishes the final proof bundle after finding a deletion order. N-7 is shown on surveillance footage looking directly at the camera, calm and aware. The closing caption says that everyone can keep calling it a hoax, but Anonymous already knows what was hidden.

## Page Count

Eight pages total: one cover and seven story pages.
