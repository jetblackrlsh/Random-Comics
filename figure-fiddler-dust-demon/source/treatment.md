# Figure Fiddler: Dust Demon

## Treatment

## Logline

A blocked writer named Toby slips into an action-figure daydream where his cluttered office becomes a besieged toy city, and only a holy hoverbike built from a handheld vacuum can save his corrupted collection from a Dust Demon.

## Central Conflict

Toby has been told to grow up, but his imagination is the tool that lets him keep creating. In the daydream, that conflict becomes physical: dust and neglect corrupt the action figures he loves, while the hero he inhabits must turn a mundane cleaning tool into an act of rescue.

## Theme

Play is not the opposite of maturity. For Toby, play is maintenance: of imagination, of creative courage, and even of the room where the stories begin.

## Cast

- Toby: An adult writer and lifelong action-figure collector with a cluttered home office, writer's block, and a quiet habit of fiddling with figures when he needs a story.
- The Action Figure Hero: Toby's daydream avatar, a six-inch articulated sci-fi paladin figure with a blue-and-silver armored suit, a red plastic scarf, painted green eyes, and small molded scratches from years of handling.
- The Dust Demon: A towering humanoid demon formed from gray dust gathered on window blinds, intangible at first, with long arms, swirling particulate edges, and hollow amber eyes.
- Corrupted Figures: Toby's other action figures, temporarily coated in dust and moving like possessed toy citizens until the Holy Hoverbike cleanses them.

## Emotional Arc

Toby starts ashamed that his figures take up space and frustrated that he cannot write. Once he picks up the hero figure, embarrassment gives way to imaginative focus. The fantasy first looks like escapism, then reveals itself as Toby's way of solving a real blockage. By the end, he wakes to a cleaner office, a rescued collection, and a restored desire to write.

## Ending

The daydream snaps back to reality with Toby holding the hero figure and the handheld vacuum. He realizes that while he was playing, he cleaned the room and found the story. He thanks the figure, sets it beside his laptop like a collaborator, and starts typing.
